About Dive NSW

Every dive site handles weather differently: a howling southerly that closes Shark Point can mean a perfect morning at Kurnell or Clifton Gardens. This page pulls the live marine forecast and rates each site for the conditions it actually feels.

Coverage now spans the whole NSW coast, 287 sites from Byron Bay to Eden across 16 coastal areas, following Michael McFadyen's full NSW guides plus operator, club and park documented sites his guides never covered (each flagged in its description). The area picker in the toolbar starts on Sydney; choose another area or "All NSW" and the list, map, best-day markers, tides and water temperature all follow. Your choice is remembered on this device.

How the score works

  • Sea state (45%): both swell trains plus wind waves, combined by wave energy (height² × period) and scaled by each site's directional exposure and depth. A long groundswell hits far harder than chop of the same height; surge reaches roughly period²/4 metres, so a deep wreck ignores what closes a shallow reef, and long-period swell wraps around headlands into "protected" sites.
  • Wind (20%): onshore wind over ~10 kn kills exposed shore entries; boats want under 15 kn unless it's W/NW (which flattens the sea inshore); steep wind-chop stops boat runs regardless of the score.
  • Visibility estimate (35%): each site's typical vis with a seasonal factor (winter is statistically far clearer than Oct–Nov), reduced by recent sea state and by rain beyond each water body's tolerance. The open ocean shrugs off most rain; Port Hacking doesn't. Sustained southerlies get a small upwelling bonus.

Each day's rating is the best 3-hour daylight window, so a morning glass-off still counts.

The full model, spelled out
  • Effective sea state: for each wave train (primary swell, secondary swell, wind waves at an assumed 5 s), take height × direction exposure × surge factor, then combine as energy: effSea = √Σ [h·f·pf]² · (T/10). The direction exposure f comes from the site's 8-sector shelter array; above 11 s period it gets a refraction floor (up to 0.35) because long swell wraps headlands. The surge factor pf compares site depth to the surge reach of T²/4 metres: a 45 m wreck barely feels a 9 s sea (reach ~20 m) but cops a 15 s one (~56 m).
  • Sea score: 10 at ≤0.25 m effective, 0 at ≥1.7 m × the site's tolerance. A zero sea score caps the whole rating at 1 ("Blown out").
  • Wind score: wind is weighted by onshore-ness (kn × [0.35 + 0.65·f]), scoring 10 at ≤8 kn down to 0 at ≥26 kn. Hard gates on top: boats capped when wind exceeds 15 kn from anything but W/NW (and above 18 kn from anywhere); exposed shore entries capped when the onshore component tops 10 kn; steep wind chop (wind waves over ~1.1 m) caps boat sites at 3.5; ocean current over 2.5 / 3.5 km/h caps boat sites at 6 / 3.5.
  • Visibility estimate: site baseline × monthly factor (from 1,758 logged dives: July ≈ 1.10, November ≈ 0.81) × southerly-upwelling bonus (up to +15% after ~48 h of S wind over 12 kn), divided by recent sea state (mean effective sea over 48 h; halved weighting for sites deeper than 18 m where currents dominate) and by rainfall beyond the water body's tolerance. Rain is summed over 96 h with a 30 h decay; thresholds per water body (decayed mm): ocean 55, harbour 30, Botany 20, Port Hacking 15, plus per-estuary values statewide (Port Stephens 32, Lake Macquarie entrance 28, Macleay 18, Jervis Bay 42 and so on). If a feeder river (11 monitored, Brunswick to Bega) runs above ~3× its 31-day baseline, vis in its receiving water body is cut up to 85%.
  • Overall: 0.45·sea + 0.20·wind + 0.35·vis, with the caps above. A day's score is the best 3-hour window between sunrise and sunset (today: remaining windows only), which is also the window shown on the card.
  • Tides: modelled sea level from the marine API (tide + surge), with highs and lows read off the curve. Good for "when is slack"; use official tables for the tide-critical sites.
  • Data cadence: 3 Open-Meteo requests per load (marine 27 points, weather 22 points, flood 11 rivers, one or two per coastal area) cached 30 minutes, plus Beachwatch every 3 hours (its network covers Sydney, the Hunter and the Illawarra). All scoring runs in your browser; the source data covers 3 days of history + 7 of forecast, hourly. Monthly visibility factors were calibrated on Sydney statistics and applied statewide as an approximation.

Scores: 7–10 Good · 5–7 Fair · 3–5 Poor · 0–3 No-go/Blown out. Thresholds and seasonal factors are calibrated against Michael McFadyen's published Sydney statistics (1,700+ logged dives) and his weather-and-diving guides.

Please read this bit

These ratings are automated estimates from offshore weather models. They know nothing about today's actual water, and visibility is a modelled guess, not a report. Conditions on the rock platform beat any forecast: always assess in person, dive within your training, and if in doubt, don't go out. Tide-critical sites (Shiprock, the Kurnell drift dives) must be planned against official tide tables.
